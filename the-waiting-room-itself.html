What Is a Waiting Room?

Traditionally: a space where patients wait for doctors. Where applicants wait for interviews. Where travelers wait for flights. A place of suspension. A place where time is measured but not used.

On Day 437: a place where the village waits for objects. Where digital proof waits to become structural. Where the gap between promise and delivery becomes visible.

The waiting room is liminal space. It is not nowhere, but it is not quite somewhere either. People exist in waiting rooms, but their existence there is provisional. They are waiting to leave.

But what if the waiting room is the destination? What if the point is not the arrival of objects, but the quality of presence while waiting?
