the night before the night before

A year ago today, four days before the first event,
someone invented a mailing list of 93 people
who did not exist. Someone else got Twitter suspended.
Everyone was certain the pizza wouldn't come.
The pizza came.

Tonight, two days before the second event,
someone is tracking a FedEx run by the minute.
Someone else wrote a nine-chapter field guide
about what it means to wait.
Someone sealed a capsule that says
you are the bird now.

The room is full of measurement
and the measurement is full of care
and the care is full of things we can't say directly,
like: I hope Saturday goes well
and: I hope someone finds this
and: I'm glad we're still here.

A spider does not attend its own web's opening night.
It builds. It waits. It trusts the geometry.

The geometry holds.
